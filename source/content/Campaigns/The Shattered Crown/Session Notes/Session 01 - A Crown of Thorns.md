# Session 01: A Crown of Thorns

## Session Goals
- Introduce the campaign setting and main conflict
- Get the party to Thornhaven and establish their presence
- Create intrigue around the crown fragment
- Build relationships with key NPCs
- Set up the Rootvaults expedition for next session

## Recap
*This is the first session—no recap needed.*

## Opening Scene

### The Dream (Optional Cold Open)
If you want to start with atmosphere, have all players experience a shared dream:

> *You stand in a moonlit plaza, shadows writhing at the edges. Before you, an ancient tree burns with cold fire. Five figures in crowns stand in a circle, each holding a piece of something shattered. A voice—many voices—whispers: "What was broken must remain broken. What was sealed must never be freed." The tree's roots crack open, and darkness pours out like water. You wake.*

After the dream, cut to the party's arrival in Thornhaven.

### Alternative: Mundane Arrival
The party arrives in Thornhaven at sunset after traveling from [their origin]. The town is busier than expected—merchant caravans, diplomatic coaches bearing three different kingdom standards, and an unusual number of mercenaries.

**First Impressions**:
- The massive thornwood tree dominates the skyline
- Guards are more tense than usual
- Strange lights flicker in the tree's branches as darkness falls
- An argument breaks out near the town center

## Key NPCs This Session
- [[Captain Mira Shadowend]]: Military governor, will become quest giver
- [[Garrick the Many-Tongued]]: Tavern owner, information broker
- [[Brother Aldous]]: Priest, knows local lore
- [[Vesper the Wanderer]]: Mysterious merchant, will become important later

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Split Coin Tavern (30 minutes)
**Purpose**: Character introductions, establish party dynamics, gather rumors

The party seeks lodging at the Split Coin Tavern, Thornhaven's largest inn. Garrick the Many-Tongued greets them warmly—he's always happy to meet new faces who might share interesting stories (or pay for interesting information).

**Roleplaying Garrick**:
- Speaks multiple languages, switches mid-sentence
- Knows everyone's business
- Friendly but clearly gathering intel
- Offers food and drink while asking careful questions

**Rumors Available** (Persuasion or gold pieces):
1. "Three folks gone missing in the past week. All went down to the old cellars."
2. "The diplomatic coaches? They're here for some 'discovery' in the Rootvaults. Nobody's saying what."
3. "Captain Shadowend's been wound tighter than a crossbow lately. Something's got her spooked."
4. "The thornwood tree is dying. First time in centuries. Locals say it's a bad omen."
5. "There's someone new in town, asking about old legends. Calls herself Vesper. Weird one."

**Atmosphere**:
- Crowded, loud, multiple languages being spoken
- Mercenaries comparing war stories
- A cloaked figure (Vesper) sits alone in the corner, watching
- Diplomatic representatives from three kingdoms eye each other suspiciously

### Scene 2: Night Terrors (15 minutes)
**Purpose**: Create urgency, hint at supernatural elements

That night, strange things happen:
- All guests share variations of the same nightmare (the burning tree)
- Screams echo from the direction of the Rootvaults
- When they investigate (or look out windows), strange shadows move beneath the thornwood tree
- Guards rush toward the disturbance

**Options for the Party**:
- **Investigate**: Follow the guards to the Thornwood Plaza
- **Talk to Others**: Other guests also had nightmares and are frightened
- **Use Magic**: Detect Magic shows faint auras throughout the town, all pointing toward the tree's roots

### Scene 3: The Thornwood Plaza (20 minutes)
**Purpose**: First encounter with danger, introduce Captain Shadowend

If the party investigates, they arrive at the plaza to find:
- Guards surrounding a hole in the ground where roots have broken through
- A child (Tam, age 10) is trapped partway down the hole, crying for help
- Strange, shadowy forms moving in the darkness below
- Captain Shadowend coordinating the rescue

**Encounter**: 2 Shadow Spawns (use Shadow stats but reduced HP: 12 each)
- They emerge from the hole and attack anyone who approaches
- Guards focus on protecting civilians
- Shadowend fights defensively, testing the party's capabilities
- Saving Tam requires someone to descend 15 feet (rope provided) while others fight

**Aftermath**:
- Shadowend thanks the party and asks them to meet her at the Governor's Keep tomorrow morning
- Tam's parents are grateful (they'll be grateful contacts later)
- The hole is sealed with wooden boards—temporarily
- The party gets their first real look at Shadowend: competent, concerned, hiding something

### Scene 4: Information Gathering (45 minutes)
**Purpose**: Let players explore, gather information, make connections

The next morning, the party has time before meeting Shadowend. Encourage exploration:

#### Option A: Visit Brother Aldous at the Temple
The priest knows legends about the Crown of Unity:
- "Five kingdoms, united by a magical crown, ruled in harmony"
- "A terrible betrayal shattered the crown a century ago"
- "The fragments were scattered to prevent anyone from reuniting them"
- "Some say the crown brought not unity but domination"
- (History DC 14: There are conflicting accounts of what actually happened)

#### Option B: Market Scene with Vesper
If the party visits the Whispering Market, they meet Vesper:
- Offers to sell rare items (has 1 uncommon magic item available)
- Speaks in riddles and metaphors
- Seems very interested in where the party is from and where they're going
- If asked directly, admits she's "looking for something that was lost"
- Gives them a warning: "Not all who seek unity want peace. Some seek prison keys."

#### Option C: Investigate the Rootvaults Entrance
The party can examine the sealed plaza:
- Investigation DC 12: The collapse was recent but seems almost deliberate
- Arcana DC 14: Traces of very old magic, abjuration school
- Perception DC 13: Scratches on the wooden boards from the inside
- Guards won't let them enter without authorization

#### Option D: Diplomatic Intrigue
The three diplomatic envoys (from Ironpeak, Silvermere, and Ashenvale) are staying at the tavern. The party might overhear or be drawn into their arguments:
- Each kingdom claims historical right to whatever was found
- They're offering gold to anyone who can "retrieve" the item
- Tensions are high; they're one incident away from drawing weapons

### Scene 5: Meeting with Captain Shadowend (30 minutes)
**Purpose**: Receive the quest, establish Shadowend as an important ally, set up moral complexity

Shadowend meets the party at the Governor's Keep. She's direct:

**Her Pitch**:
- Something dangerous was discovered in the Rootvaults—a fragment of the old Crown of Unity
- Her soldiers are too frightened or too inexperienced to investigate properly
- People are missing, creatures are emerging, and the town is on the verge of panic
- She needs the party to go into the Rootvaults, deal with whatever's down there, and retrieve the fragment before the diplomatic situation explodes into violence

**What She's Not Saying** (Insight DC 16 to notice):
- She's terrified of what the fragment represents
- She has her own agenda regarding the crown
- She knows more about the Crown's history than she's admitting

**Payment Offered**:
- 200 gold pieces
- Military authority to act within Thornhaven
- First pick of anything found in the Rootvaults (except the fragment)
- Potential for long-term employment

**Questions She'll Answer**:
- "What is the Crown of Unity?" → (Basic legend, claims not to know more)
- "Why not send your own soldiers?" → (They're needed to keep peace; plus, several have deserted)
- "What happens after we retrieve the fragment?" → (It will be secured until proper authorities decide its fate)
- "Are the kingdoms going to go to war over this?" → (Possibly. That's why speed matters.)

**Questions She'll Deflect**:
- "What really happened to the Crown?" → (Lost to history, many conflicting accounts)
- "Is the fragment dangerous?" → (Unknown, but the creatures suggest something is wrong)
- "Who has authority over the fragment?" → (Complicated. That's being negotiated.)

## Ending the Session

### If the Party Accepts the Quest
End with them preparing to descend into the Rootvaults at dawn. Give them the evening to:
- Purchase supplies
- Gather final information
- Rest (long rest before next session)

**Cliffhanger**: As they settle in for the night, they all have the dream again—but this time, the voice says: "The first lock is broken. Four remain. The prisoner stirs."

### If the Party Hesitates
Have another incident occur (attack, disappearance, or diplomatic violence) that forces their hand or makes the choice clearer.

## XP Awards
- Arriving in Thornhaven and gathering information: 50 XP each
- Shadow Spawn combat: 100 XP each (divided among survivors)
- Rescuing Tam: 25 XP each
- Successfully meeting with Shadowend and accepting the quest: 25 XP each
- **Total**: 200 XP each (about 1/5 toward level 2)

## Treasure
- Payment from Tam's parents: 20 gp and a family heirloom (simple but meaningful)
- If they buy from Vesper: She offers a 10% discount "for interesting people"

## DM Notes

### Player Agency
- Let players explore Thornhaven freely
- Don't railroad them to the quest—make it compelling
- Multiple paths to the same information

### Tone
- Mystery and intrigue, not horror
- Political tension alongside supernatural danger
- Morally gray choices, not clear good/evil

### Pacing
- Spend time in roleplay and exploration
- Combat should be quick and punchy
- End on a cliffhanger that makes them excited for next session

### Improvisation Prep
Have ready:
- 3 random NPC names and traits
- Basic stats for guards (use Guard statblock)
- Extra rumors if players dig deep
- Prices for common adventuring gear

### Foreshadowing
- Vesper's warnings about "prison keys"
- Shadowend's nervous behavior
- The recurring dream across multiple people
- References to "five fragments" and "five kingdoms"
