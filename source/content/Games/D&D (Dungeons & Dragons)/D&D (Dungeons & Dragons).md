Dungeons & Dragons is the world's most popular tabletop roleplaying game, published by [Wizards of the Coast](https://www.dndbeyond.com). Players create characters and embark on adventures led by a Dungeon Master (DM) who narrates the world, controls enemies, and adjudicates the rules. The game blends storytelling, strategy, and improvisation — from dungeon crawls and dragon fights to political intrigue and mystery.

## About the Game

**Publisher:** Wizards of the Coast  
**Edition:** 5th Edition (5e)  
**Scale:** Roleplaying — typically 3–6 players plus a Dungeon Master  
**Session Length:** Usually 2–4 hours  
**Core Mechanic:** Roll a d20, add relevant modifiers, and meet or beat a Difficulty Class (DC) or the target's Armour Class (AC)

Each character has six core ability scores — Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma — which feed into skills, saving throws, and attack rolls. This system is flexible enough to handle almost any situation while keeping the flow of play intuitive.

## Core Rules

*These are based on D&D 5th Edition (2024 revision)*

#### Ability Checks
When a character attempts something with a chance of failure, the DM calls for an ability check. Roll a d20 and add the relevant ability modifier (and proficiency bonus if applicable). Meet or beat the DC to succeed.

| Difficulty | DC |
| ---------- | -- |
| Very Easy  | 5  |
| Easy       | 10 |
| Medium     | 15 |
| Hard       | 20 |
| Very Hard  | 25 |
| Nearly Impossible | 30 |

#### Combat

**Initiative:** At the start of combat, all combatants roll d20 + Dexterity modifier. Turns proceed from highest to lowest.

**On Your Turn:** Each combatant gets one Action, one Bonus Action (if available), and movement up to their speed.

| Action         | Description                                                   |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Attack         | Make one or more weapon or spell attacks                      |
| Cast a Spell   | Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action                 |
| Dash           | Double your movement speed this turn                          |
| Disengage      | Move without provoking opportunity attacks                    |
| Dodge          | Attackers have disadvantage; you have advantage on Dex saves |
| Help           | Give an ally advantage on their next check or attack         |
| Hide           | Make a Stealth check to become hidden                         |
| Ready          | Prepare an action to trigger on a specific condition          |

#### Attack Rolls & Damage
Roll d20 + attack modifier vs target's AC. On a hit, roll the weapon or spell's damage dice and add the relevant modifier. A natural 20 is a critical hit — roll all damage dice twice.

#### Saving Throws
When subjected to harmful effects, roll d20 + the relevant saving throw modifier vs the effect's DC. Success usually means reduced or avoided harm.

#### Advantage & Disadvantage
Roll two d20s and take the higher (advantage) or lower (disadvantage) result. These never stack — you either have advantage, disadvantage, or neither.

#### Conditions
Conditions alter a creature's capabilities. Common ones include:

| Condition     | Key Effect                                                        |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Blinded       | Auto-fail sight checks; attacks against you have advantage        |
| Charmed       | Can't attack the charmer; charmer has advantage on social checks  |
| Frightened    | Disadvantage on checks/attacks while source is in sight           |
| Grappled      | Speed becomes 0                                                   |
| Incapacitated | Can't take actions or reactions                                   |
| Paralyzed     | Incapacitated; auto-fail Str/Dex saves; hits within 5ft are crits |
| Poisoned      | Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks                   |
| Prone         | Disadvantage on attacks; melee hits are crits, ranged have disadv |
| Restrained    | Speed 0; disadvantage on attacks and Dex saves                    |
| Stunned       | Incapacitated; auto-fail Str/Dex saves; attacks against have adv  |
| Unconscious   | Paralyzed; auto-fail Str/Dex saves; falls prone                   |

#### Death & Dying
When a character drops to 0 HP they fall unconscious and begin making **death saving throws** at the start of each turn — roll d20, 10+ is a success. Three successes = stabilised; three failures = dead. A natural 1 counts as two failures; a natural 20 restores 1 HP. Any damage while at 0 HP counts as a failure (massive damage may kill outright).

#### Resting
- **Short Rest (1 hour):** Spend Hit Dice to recover HP; some class features recharge.  
- **Long Rest (8 hours):** Recover all HP, half your Hit Dice, and most expended spell slots and class features.
