# Campaign Quick Reference

## Campaign Elevator Pitch
A century ago, five heroes shattered the Crown of Unity to imprison the Hollow King—an entity born of rage and pain. Now the fragments are awakening, and multiple factions race to control them. The party must uncover the truth, navigate political intrigue, and choose whether to restore, destroy, or transcend the Crown's legacy.

---

## Core Themes
1. **Broken Trust**: Is unity worth the cost? Can divided peoples work together?
2. **Hidden History**: The truth is buried under lies. Which version is real?
3. **Power's Price**: Every great magic demands sacrifice. What will you pay?
4. **Unity Through Adversity**: The threat everyone shares might be what finally unites them

---

## Key Campaign Questions
- What really happened during the Shattering?
- Can the Hollow King be redeemed instead of defeated?
- Will the party choose unity, division, or a third path?
- What price is worth paying for peace?

---

## The Three Paths (Potential Endings)

### Path 1: Reunification
**Outcome**: Crown is reassembled, Hollow King is freed
**Consequence**: Must defeat the Hollow King in combat or negotiate
**Theme**: Face the past directly, even if dangerous

### Path 2: Permanent Division
**Outcome**: Fragments are destroyed, Crown can never return
**Consequence**: Kingdoms remain divided, but threat is ended
**Theme**: Safety through separation, but never true unity

### Path 3: Transcendence (Hidden)
**Outcome**: Heal the Hollow King and transform the Crown's purpose
**Consequence**: Requires all five kingdoms to choose unity willingly
**Theme**: Break the cycle, find new meaning in old magic
**How**: Discover that the Hollow King can be redeemed through collective choice rather than force

## Campaign Structure Overview

### Act 1: The Gathering Storm (Levels 1-3)
**Focus**: Introduce world, get first fragment, meet factions
**Location**: [[Thornhaven]] and surroundings
**Key Quest**: [[Quest - The Fragment of Thornhaven]]
**Revelation**: The Crown might not be what everyone thinks

### Act 2: The Five Kingdoms (Levels 4-6)
**Focus**: Travel through kingdoms, gather fragments, build alliances
**Locations**: All five kingdoms
**Key Quests**: Political missions, fragment retrieval
**Revelation**: The Veilwalkers' true nature, deeper conspiracy

### Act 3: The Truth Revealed (Levels 7-8)
**Focus**: Discover the Hollow King, understand the Shattering
**Locations**: [[The Shattered Vault]], hidden history sites
**Key Quest**: [[Quest - The Traitor's Legacy]]
**Revelation**: The heroes sacrificed themselves; the Crown was a prison

### Act 4: The Final Choice (Levels 9-10)
**Focus**: Unite the kingdoms or face division, final confrontation
**Locations**: [[The Crown Chamber]], [[The Heart of the World]]
**Key Quest**: [[Quest - The Reunification]]
**Resolution**: Party's choice determines ending

## Faction Quick Reference

### The Crown's Keepers
**Goal**: Keep fragments separated
**Methods**: Secrecy, sabotage, protection
**Leader**: High Keeper Aldric (mysterious)
**Party Contact**: Captain Shadowend, Vesper

### The Veilwalkers
**Goal**: Reunite the Crown (unknowingly freeing the Hollow King)
**Methods**: Theft, assassination, possession
**Leader**: The Veilwalker Prime
**Motivation**: Corrupted loyalty to their transformed masters

### The Unification Front
**Goal**: Reunite the Crown, believing it will bring peace
**Methods**: Diplomacy, public pressure, archaeological expeditions
**Leader**: Various idealistic nobles
**Danger**: Useful idiots who could trigger disaster

### The Silent Consortium
**Goal**: Preserve knowledge, prevent panic
**Methods**: Information control, scholarly research
**Leader**: Council of Archivists
**Party Contact**: Professor Grimward

## Session Zero Questions for Players

### Character Creation Prompts
1. How does your character feel about authority and kingdoms?
2. What's your character's relationship with ancient history/magic?
3. Would your character value unity or independence more?
4. What would your character sacrifice for peace?

### Table Expectations
- This campaign involves moral ambiguity—no clear "right" answers
- Political intrigue will be significant alongside combat
- Player choices will genuinely affect the ending
- Some NPCs will lie or hide information (in character, not adversarial)

## Improvisation Tools

### Random NPC Generator
1. Roll occupation: Guard, Merchant, Scholar, Farmer, Artisan, Refugee
2. Roll personality: Helpful, Suspicious, Afraid, Greedy, Idealistic, Tired
3. Roll connection: Neutral, Pro-Unity, Pro-Division, Faction Spy, Hiding Something

### Random Complication
1. Veilwalker scout spotted
2. Diplomatic incident occurs
3. NPC turns out to be more important than seemed
4. Weather turns dangerous
5. Unexpected ally appears
6. Faction makes move on fragment

### Mystery/Investigation Clues
Always have three paths to each important clue:
- **Direct**: Investigation/Perception check
- **Social**: Talking to right NPC
- **Magic**: Detect Magic, Divination, etc.

## Tone and Atmosphere

### This Campaign IS:
- Morally complex political fantasy
- Mystery with escalating stakes
- Character-driven with big world events
- About choice and consequence

### This Campaign IS NOT:
- Pure dungeon crawl
- Black-and-white good vs evil
- Railroad to predetermined ending
- Horror or grimdark

### Mood Keywords
Investigation, intrigue, ancient secrets, difficult choices, epic scale, personal stakes

## Useful Session Structures

### Investigation Session
1. Present mystery/problem
2. Let players explore freely
3. Ensure 3+ clues are findable
4. NPC interaction scene
5. Revelation or complication
6. Setup next step

### Travel Session
1. Travel montage with one challenge
2. Arrive at destination
3. First impressions of new location
4. Meet local contact/authority
5. Learn local situation
6. Present local quest hook

### Dungeon Session
1. Entrance and atmosphere
2. 2-3 combat encounters
3. 1-2 puzzles or challenges
4. Lore/discovery element
5. Boss or major decision
6. Consequences and rewards

### Diplomatic Session
1. Establish stakes and tensions
2. Multiple factions present
3. Let players navigate socially
4. Complicate with new information
5. Force a choice or alliance
6. Show consequences of choice

## Emergency Session Prep

If you need to prep a session quickly:

1. **Where are they?** Location from campaign
2. **Who do they meet?** Pick 2 NPCs from files
3. **What happens?** One of: Fight, Chase, Mystery, Negotiation
4. **What's at stake?** Fragment, Alliance, Information, Safety
5. **How does it connect?** Link to main plot or NPC arc

## Player Handout: What Your Characters Know

- The Crown of Unity once united five kingdoms

- The kingdoms have been divided since
- Fragments are said to exist but none have been seen in a century
- Multiple kingdoms claim right to any fragments found

### What They've Learned (Session 1)
- A fragment appeared in Thornhaven's Rootvaults
- Strange creatures and disappearances followed
- Three kingdoms sent diplomats to claim it
- Captain Shadowend needs help securing it
- Something about this situation feels wrong
