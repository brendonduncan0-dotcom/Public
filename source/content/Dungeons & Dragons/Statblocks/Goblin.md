## Lore

Goblins are small, black-hearted humanoids that lair in caves, abandoned mines, despoiled dungeons, and other dismal settings. Individually weak, goblins gather in large numbers to torment other creatures. They are motivated by greed and malice, and are renowned for their cowardice in the face of superior forces.

Goblins belong to a family of creatures called goblinoids, which includes their larger cousins the hobgoblins and bugbears. They have flat faces, broad noses, pointed ears, and small, sharp fangs. Their foreheads slope back, and their eyes are usually dull and glazed. They always walk upright, but their arms hang down almost to their knees. Their skin color ranges from yellow through orange to deep red, and they are typically filthy.

Goblins live in dark, dank places where they can ambush prey and scavenge for food. They worship deities of trickery and destruction, building crude shrines in their lairs. Goblin tribes are led by the strongest among them, though leadership often changes through acts of treachery and assassination.

## Tactics

Goblins are cowardly and cunning, preferring to attack from ambush or with overwhelming numbers. They make extensive use of their Nimble Escape feature, allowing them to dart in and out of combat without provoking opportunity attacks. A typical goblin will fire its shortbow from range, using cover whenever possible, and only engage in melee when cornered or when they have a significant numerical advantage.

When fighting in groups, goblins coordinate to focus fire on isolated or wounded targets. They're quick to flee when the battle turns against them, often leaving slower members of their tribe behind as distractions. Goblins set simple traps around their lairs—pit traps, tripwires connected to bells, or precariously balanced objects—to give them warning and slow down intruders.

Smart goblin leaders position ranged attackers on elevated terrain or behind barricades while melee goblins harass from the flanks. If forced to retreat, they'll collapse tunnels, bar doors, or release captured animals to cover their escape.

## Encounter Ideas

**Ambush at the Crossroads**: A band of 6-8 goblins has been waylaying travelers at a forest crossroads. They've built crude barricades from fallen logs and positioned archers in trees. The goblins demand "shiny things" as toll, but their leader carries a stolen signet ring that belongs to a local noble—recovering it could earn the party a reward and gratitude.

**The Stolen Supplies**: Goblins have been raiding a remote farmstead nightly, stealing chickens, tools, and food. The party is hired to track them back to their cave lair. Inside, they discover the goblins are stockpiling supplies for a larger force of bugbears who plan to raid the nearby village. The party must decide whether to eliminate the threat quietly or warn the village.

**Goblin Diplomacy**: A goblin scout approaches the party with a white flag (actually a dirty rag), claiming his tribe wants to make a deal. Their warren has been invaded by giant spiders, and they'll pay the party in "shinies and secrets" to clear them out. The goblins can provide information about other dungeon areas, but can they be trusted to honor their bargain?
