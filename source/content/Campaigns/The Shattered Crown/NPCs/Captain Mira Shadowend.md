# Captain Mira Shadowend

## Quick Reference
**Role**: Military Governor of Thornhaven  
**First Appearance**: [[Session 01 - A Crown of Thorns]]  
**Voice**: Clipped, military precision with occasional dry humor  
**Motivation**: Protect Thornhaven and prevent war, no matter the cost

## Description
Mira is a tall woman with close-cropped dark hair, a jagged scar across her left cheek, and eyes that miss nothing. She wears practical military attire—reinforced leather with minimal decoration—and always keeps her sword within reach. Her movements are economical and controlled, the hallmark of a career soldier.

## Personality
- **Pragmatic**: Makes hard choices without hesitation
- **Burden-Bearer**: Feels personally responsible for every citizen's safety
- **Haunted**: Carries secrets that wake her in the night
- **Dry Wit**: Uses humor to deflect from emotional topics

## Background
Mira served in the Ironpeak military for fifteen years before taking the Thornhaven posting three years ago. What most don't know is that she was once a Crown's Keeper—trained from youth to guard against the fragments ever being reunited. She failed to prevent the Thornhaven fragment's discovery, and that failure weighs heavily on her conscience.

Her mentor, Lord Commander [[Revan Shadowend]] (her adoptive father), died protecting the secret of the fragments' locations. His last words to her were "Protect the ignorance." Now she must decide whether to maintain the lie or trust the party with the truth.

## Secrets
- **Secret 1**: Former Crown's Keeper who knows the truth about the Crown's purpose
- **Secret 2**: Has a dead drop communication method with other Keepers
- **Secret 3**: Her real name is Mira Thornbridge—she took "Shadowend" after Revan's death
- **Secret 4**: Has been secretly sabotaging diplomatic efforts to claim the fragment
- **Secret 5**: Knows the location of two other fragments

## Goals & Motivations
**Primary Goal**: Keep the fragments separated at all costs  
**Secondary Goal**: Protect Thornhaven from becoming a war zone  
**Personal Goal**: Honor Revan's sacrifice by finishing what he started  
**Secret Agenda**: May need to destroy the Thornhaven fragment if it can't be secured

## Relationships
- **[[Vesper the Wanderer]]**: Secret ally, also a Crown's Keeper
- **[[Queen Aldara the Just]]**: Former commander who knows nothing of Mira's true mission
- **[[The Veilwalker Prime]]**: Unknown enemy hunting the same fragments
- **[[Garrick the Many-Tongued]]**: Useful but untrustworthy information source
- **The Party**: Potential assets or dangerous variables—time will tell

## Roleplaying Notes

### Dialogue Quirks
- Addresses people by title or role ("Scholar," "Merchant," "Adventurer")
- Uses military terminology even in casual conversation
- Phrases things as orders softened into requests
- When stressed: "Soldier on" or "Keep formation"

### Key Phrases
- "I've seen what happens when good intentions meet bad timing."
- "Thornhaven doesn't need heroes. It needs people who'll do what's necessary."
- "The truth is a luxury we can't afford right now."
- "I won't let this town become a battlefield. Not on my watch."

### Character Arc
- **Act 1**: Distrustful authority figure who gives the party minimal information
- **Act 2**: Forced to work with the party when Veilwalkers attack; begins to respect them
- **Act 3**: Reveals the truth about the Crown and her role as a Keeper; asks for help
- **Act 4**: Must choose between her oath and what the party believes is right

## Plot Hooks
- Can provide the party with military resources and authority
- Has information about the Crown's history if persuaded to share
- Becomes target of Veilwalker assassination attempts
- Eventual mentor figure who teaches them about the weight of leadership

## Secrets to Reveal
- **Session 3-4**: She's been delaying diplomatic progress intentionally
- **Session 8-10**: She's a Crown's Keeper and knows the truth
- **Session 15+**: Her real name and relationship to Revan Shadowend
- **Act 3**: The locations of two other fragments

## Props & Handouts

- Wears a signet ring with the Crown's Keeper symbol (obscured by wear)
