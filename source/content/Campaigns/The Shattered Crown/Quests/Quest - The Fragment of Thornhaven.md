# Quest: The Fragment of Thornhaven

## Quest Type
Main Story Quest (Act 1 Introduction)

## Level Range
1-2 (This should be the party's introduction to the campaign)

## Overview
The party arrives in [[Thornhaven]] just as a section of the ancient Rootvaults has collapsed, revealing a sealed chamber containing a fragment of the legendary Crown of Unity. Strange occurrences plague the town, people are disappearing, and three kingdoms each claim ownership of the artifact. The party must investigate the Rootvaults, survive its dangers, and make a crucial choice about the fragment's fate.

## Quest Giver
[[Captain Mira Shadowend]] approaches the party after hearing of their capabilities (or the party seeks her out after witnessing strange events)

## The Hook
Choose one based on party background:

### Hook 1: The Nightmare
The party awakens from a shared nightmare showing the thornwood tree burning and a crown shattering into pieces. A voice whispers: "Return what was broken. Thornhaven holds the key."

### Hook 2: The Hire
Captain Shadowend hires the party to investigate the Rootvaults after her soldiers refuse to go deeper—those who have, haven't returned.

### Hook 3: The Rescue
A local child went missing in the Rootvaults. The party volunteers to find them and discovers far more than they bargained for.

## Quest Stages

### Stage 1: Arrival and Investigation
**Objectives:**
- Arrive in Thornhaven and learn about recent events
- Interview witnesses about the disappearances
- Investigate the Thornwood Plaza and gather rumors
- Meet Captain Shadowend and receive official sanction to explore the Rootvaults

**Key NPCs**: [[Captain Mira Shadowend]], [[Garrick the Many-Tongued]], [[Brother Aldous]]

**Investigation Reveals**:
- Three people have gone missing in the past week
- All disappeared near the collapsed section of Rootvaults
- Strange sounds echo from below at night
- The diplomatic envoys are extremely interested in the "discovery"
- Local scholars mention legends of the Crown of Unity

**Skill Challenges**:
- **Persuasion/Intimidation DC 12**: Get locals to talk about what they've seen
- **Investigation DC 14**: Find evidence of struggle near the Rootvault entrance
- **History DC 16**: Recall legends about the Crown of Unity

### Stage 2: Into the Rootvaults
**Objectives:**
- Descend into the Rootvaults
- Navigate the ancient root system
- Defeat or evade the creatures that have been awakened
- Reach the sealed chamber

**Dungeon Features**:
- **Bioluminescent Fungi**: Provides dim light; disturbing them alerts creatures
- **Root Bridges**: Crossing requires Acrobatics DC 12 or risk falling
- **Ancient Wards**: Glowing runes that Detect Magic reveals as abjuration
- **Echoing Halls**: Sound carries unnaturally; advantage on Perception but enemies also hear the party

**Encounters**:
1. **Root Blights** (3): Awakened plant creatures defending their territory
2. **Shadow Spawns** (4): Manifestations of the fragment's corrupting influence
3. **The Lost** (3 humans, mind-controlled): The missing townspeople, attacking mindlessly

**Environmental Hazards**:
- **Collapsing Roots**: DEX save DC 13 or take 2d6 bludgeoning damage
- **Spore Cloud**: CON save DC 12 or poisoned for 1 hour
- **Nightmare Visions**: WIS save DC 13 or frightened until end of next turn

### Stage 3: The Sealed Chamber
**Objectives:**
- Bypass the ancient seals protecting the chamber
- Confront the Fragment Guardian
- Secure the crown fragment
- Escape before the chamber collapses

**The Chamber**:
A circular room with walls covered in ancient murals depicting the Crown's creation and destruction. At the center, on a pedestal, sits a glowing shard of what was once a crown—the Fragment of Unity. It pulses with unnatural light.

**Murals Reveal** (with Investigation DC 14 or History DC 16):
- The Crown being forged by five monarchs
- The Crown being worn by a shadowy figure
- A great battle with the figure at its center
- The Crown shattering into five pieces
- Five heroes each taking a fragment and fleeing in different directions

**The Guardian**: [[Fragment Guardian - Thornhaven]]
A spectral champion, one of the original heroes, bound to protect the fragment. It will test the party to see if they're worthy. If they fight, it's a challenging encounter. If they parley (Persuasion DC 15), they might learn valuable information.

**Guardian's Challenge**:
- "Do you seek to unite or to divide?"
- "Will you preserve peace or pursue power?"
- "Can you bear the weight of truth?"

**The Twist**: Touching the fragment causes a vision of the Hollow King—an entity of pure darkness—sealed within the Crown. The party sees glimpses of the truth: the Crown wasn't meant to unite; it was a prison.

### Stage 4: The Escape and Choice
**Objectives:**
- Escape the collapsing Rootvaults
- Decide what to do with the fragment
- Deal with the consequences of your choice

**The Collapse**:
As soon as the fragment is removed, the seals fail and the chamber begins to collapse. The party must race through the Rootvaults (now more dangerous) while carrying the fragment.

**Skill Challenge** (3 successes before 2 failures):
- **Athletics DC 13**: Climb over collapsed sections
- **Acrobatics DC 14**: Navigate crumbling root bridges
- **Survival DC 12**: Find the fastest route out
- **Group Check DC 11**: Work together to move rubble

**Surface Consequences**:
Upon emerging, the party finds:
- Three diplomatic envoys waiting, each demanding the fragment
- Captain Shadowend suggesting it be placed in secure military custody
- A mysterious figure in the crowd ([[Vesper the Wanderer]]) who makes eye contact meaningfully
- The thornwood tree's condition has worsened

**The Choice**:
- Give the fragment to one of the three kingdoms (political favor but animosity from the others)
- Give it to Captain Shadowend (military protection but loss of control)
- Keep it themselves (independence but become targets)
- Hide it elsewhere (temporary solution but prolongs the problem)
- Attempt to study/understand it first

## Rewards

### Experience
- Stage 1: 100 XP per character
- Stage 2: 300 XP per character
- Stage 3: 400 XP per character
- Stage 4: 200 XP per character
- **Total**: 1000 XP per character (should bring level 1 characters to level 2)

### Treasure
- 150 gp in ancient coins found in the Rootvaults
- 2 potions of healing from the Guardian's chamber
- Keeper's Map: An ancient map showing the location of Thornhaven relative to "The Five Points" (vague but valuable)
- Choice of one magic item from the Guardian:
  - **Thornward Amulet**: +1 AC, grants advantage on saves against being frightened
  - **Root Whisperer's Staff**: Staff that can cast *Speak with Plants* once per day
  - **Fragment Compass**: Points toward the nearest crown fragment (subtle pull)

### Story Rewards
- Reputation in Thornhaven (opens doors)
- Captain Shadowend's guarded respect
- Knowledge of the Crown's true nature (partial)
- First crown fragment (depending on choice)
- The attention of multiple factions

## Complications & Consequences

### If they give the fragment to a kingdom:
- The other two kingdoms become hostile
- Thornhaven becomes a military target
- The party gains powerful allies but also powerful enemies

### If they give it to Captain Shadowend:
- She secretly contacts other Crown's Keepers
- The party is offered membership in the Keepers
- Diplomatic tensions ease but underground factions grow desperate

### If they keep it:
- The Veilwalkers begin hunting them specifically
- All three kingdoms send "retrieval teams"
- The fragment's presence causes strange dreams and visions
- They become sought-after by multiple factions (for better or worse)

### If they hide it:
- Temporary reprieve but future complications
- The hiding spot becomes a quest location later
- Someone will eventually find it

## DM Notes

### Pacing
- Stage 1: 1 session (investigation and roleplay)
- Stages 2-3: 1-2 sessions (dungeon exploration and combat)
- Stage 4: End of session 2 or session 3 (climactic choice)

### Flexibility
- Adjust Guardian difficulty based on party performance
- Add or remove encounters in Stage 2 based on pacing
- The "correct" choice doesn't exist—all choices have interesting consequences

### Foreshadowing
- Guardian's dialogue hints at the Crown's true nature
- Murals show the Hollow King vaguely
- Vesper's presence suggests she knows more than she's saying
- Captain Shadowend's reactions hint at her deeper knowledge

### Music & Atmosphere
- Stage 1: Mystery, tension, small-town intrigue
- Stage 2: Dungeon exploration, danger, ancient secrets
- Stage 3: Revelation, epic choice, moral ambiguity
- Stage 4: Action, consequences, new questions
