## Lore

Zylox is a plasmoid rogue operating in the Astral Sea and wildspace beyond. Like all plasmoids, Zylox is a sentient ooze capable of assuming humanoid form or flowing like liquid through the smallest cracks. Their translucent, gelatinous body shimmers with an amber hue, and they often maintain a vaguely humanoid shape with surprisingly expressive features when dealing with other creatures. Born in the depths of wildspace aboard a giff merchant vessel, Zylox learned early that their unique physiology made them perfect for infiltration, espionage, and the art of thievery.

Plasmoids like Zylox are relatively rare but increasingly common in Spelljammer ports across wildspace. They have no true homeworld, existing primarily aboard spelljamming vessels and space stations. Zylox has spent decades perfecting the art of shapeshifting to impersonate various humanoid species, slipping past security, and acquiring valuable artifacts from across the cosmos. They're particularly interested in magical items that can enhance their already formidable abilities.

Despite their roguish profession, Zylox follows a personal code: never harm innocents, and always honor contracts once made. They view theft as an art form and prefer cunning over violence. Zylox has become known in certain underworld circles of the Rock of Bral and other major ports as a reliable, if expensive, specialist for delicate acquisitions.

## Tactics

Zylox excels at infiltration and surprise attacks. Before combat begins, they use Morphic Disguise to blend in with their surroundings or impersonate someone with legitimate access to their target area. Their Amorphous trait allows them to slip through ventilation shafts, under doors, or through ship hull breaches that would be impossible for others to navigate.

In combat, Zylox relies heavily on Cunning Action to maintain mobility, using Hit and Run tactics with their shortsword or hand crossbow. They position themselves to gain advantage for Sneak Attack, often hiding behind cover or attacking from unexpected angles. When facing multiple enemies, Zylox uses Uncanny Dodge to mitigate the most dangerous attacks while retreating to a better position.

The Pseudopod Grapple is one of Zylox's signature moves—they can extend a tentacle-like appendage to grapple foes at range while still maintaining weapon attacks with their "arms." This is particularly effective on spelljamming vessels where grappling an opponent near a railing could lead to them being pushed into wildspace. If cornered, Zylox transforms into a blob form and attempts to escape through small spaces, using their Hold Breath ability to survive in hostile environments.

## Encounter Ideas

**Vault Heist in Wildspace**: The party is hired to transport a valuable artifact through wildspace when they discover their cargo has been replaced with a worthless fake. Investigation reveals Zylox infiltrated their ship by squeezing through a damaged porthole, replaced the artifact, and is now hiding somewhere aboard disguised as a crew member. The party must identify the impostor before reaching their destination, but Zylox is an expert at deflecting suspicion and creating alibis.

**Rival Operative**: The party and Zylox are both hired to acquire the same target—a set of spelljamming helm blueprints locked in an archmage's sanctum on a drifting asteroid. Rather than direct confrontation, this becomes a race to see who can infiltrate first, with both groups potentially encountering each other at inopportune moments. Zylox might offer to team up temporarily, but their true loyalty is only to the contract. Can the party trust a shapeshifting thief?

**Escape from the Brig**: Zylox has been captured and imprisoned aboard a nautiloid (mind flayer ship) after a botched infiltration attempt. They send a psychic distress beacon through an illithid tadpole they swallowed (and later killed with their acid resistance). The party intercepts the signal and must decide whether to mount a rescue. If freed, Zylox offers valuable intelligence about the mind flayers' plans and proves to be a useful ally—at least until they spot something valuable worth stealing.
