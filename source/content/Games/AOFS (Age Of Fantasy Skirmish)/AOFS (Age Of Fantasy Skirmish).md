Age of Fantasy Skirmish is a free, quick-play tabletop miniature wargame by [One Page Rules](https://onepagerules.com/games/age-of-fantasy-skirmish). Players build small warbands and clash in fast-paced battles set in a world of magic, ancient ruins, and warring factions. The rules are deliberately streamlined — the full core rules fit on a single page — making it easy to pick up and play without sacrificing tactical depth.

## About the Game

**Publisher:** One Page Rules (OPR) — rules are free to download  
**Scale:** Skirmish — typically 5–15 miniatures per side  
**Points:** Standard games run at 150–250pts  
**Table Size:** Recommended 4×4 (we play on 3×3)  
**Core Mechanic:** Roll a d6 against your unit's Quality score to hit, Defence score to survive

Each unit has just two core stats — Quality and Defence — plus weapon profiles and optional special abilities. This keeps the game fast without removing meaningful decisions.

## Core Rules

*These are based on the Core Rules v3.5.1*

#### Movement
Units must stay within 1" of at least one other member (3" when on different elevations due to falling), and within 6" of all other members. Units can only be within 1" of another unit when charging, and may only charge if at least one model is in charging distance.

| Action  | Move | Notes                |
| ------- | ---- | -------------------- |
| Charge  | 12"  | Moves into melee     |
| Rush    | 12"  | Can't shoot          |
| Advance | 6"   | Can shoot after move |
| Hold    | 0"   | Can shoot            |

#### Shooting
Models in range and line of sight may fire all ranged weapons and may fire at up to two different targets with different weapon types. Shooting models take one quality test per attack, and each success is a hit. For each hit defencing units roll one die trying to score their Defence value or higher to block, and each fail causes one wound. Then check the wound check table to see what happens.

#### Melee (Charging)
Charging models must move into base contact with targets, or as close as possible, and then the defenders do the same, moving up to 3". Models must strike enemies within 2" horizontally and 4" vertically with all Melee weapons. For each hit defencing units roll one die trying to score their Defence value or higher to block, and each fail causes one wound. Then check the wound check table to see what happens. Defenders may choose to strike back but don't have to. If one unit is destroyed, the other may move up to 3", else chargers move ack and separate by 1" if possible.

#### Fatigue
After charging, striking back or when Shaken, units hit on unmodified rolls of 6 in melee until the end of that round.

#### Wound Checks
Whenever the last model in a unit takes one or more wounds, place a wound maker next to it for each. Then the defender rolls one die, adding the number of wounds to the result.

| Roll | Effect                                                                                                                                                                             |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1-5  | **Shaken** - The model is counted as fatigued, always fails morale tests and can't contest/seize objectives. The unit must spend one full activation idle to remove the condition. |
| 6+   | **Knocked Out** - The model is removed from play.                                                                                                                                  |

Whenever a unit with multiple models takes a wound, each wound kills one model until one remains. Only the last model accumulates wounds and rolls a wound check.

*Note: For a simple way to calculate the result required to knock out a model that also works with tough, treat the wounds the model is allowed before making a wound check as hit points, then the model is then knocked out on a 5+X where X is their hit points.*

#### Moral Checks
At the end of each round, after objectives are resolved, if an army is down to half or less of its starting units, then all of its units must take a morale test. To take a morale test, take a quality test, and if failed the unit is Shaken, and if the unit was already Shaken, then it routes instead (remove from play as destroyed).

#### Push Checks
When attacking a model that is shaken, the attacker may try to push it instead. The attacker rolls one die and on a 4+, the attacker may move the model by up to 2" in any direction.

#### Jump Checks
Models may cross 1" gaps or drop up to 3" without any check but can jump or drop up to 6" with a successful Jump check. Roll X+1 dice, trying to score 3+ on each, where X is one die for every full 3" the model wants to move. If all are successes, then the model may cross the distance without counting towards its move. If any roll is failed, then the model falls from their starting position instead. All models in a unit require their own jump check.

*Note 1: A drop of up to 3" as part of your movement does not trigger fall damage as its considered a controlled movement.*

*Note 2: After a failed jump, the model is allowed one turn to regain unit coherency before being removed. When a difference in elevation is present, the unit coherency rule extends from 1" between models to 3". If unit coherency is still not achieved then the offending models are removed.

#### Falling & Fall Damage
If a model falls or is pushed off 2" tall, it takes 1 hit with AP(X) where X is AP(1) for every full 3" it fell. Then place the model within 2" of the bottom and its activation ends immediately. For units with multiple models, falling kills the model instead until only the last model remains which falls normally.

#### Climbing/Moving Up
You can climb any surface that is not completely vertical (shear); there must be a path, ladder, staircase or gentle slope.

#### Cover Terrain
Units with most models fully inside cover or behind line of sight blockers get +1 to defence rolls against shooting.

#### Difficult Terrain
Units moving through difficult terrain at any point cant move more than 6" at a time that turn.

#### Dangerous Terrain
Models moving across dangerous terrain, or that activate in it, must roll one die (or as many as their tough value), and for each roll of a 1 the unit takes a wound.
